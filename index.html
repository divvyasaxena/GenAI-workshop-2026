Program Overview

This 3-day workshop is designed to build participant capability in Generative AI through a structured, bottom-up learning pathway. The program opens with first principles - deep learning fundamentals and the evolution of the modern Generative AI landscape - before progressing to architectural intuition and practical understanding of key model families. These include adversarial approaches for visual synthesis, text-to-image generation approaches and latent workflows, and foundation models that enable text-driven and image-level intelligence across tasks.

Each module blends conceptual grounding with guided demonstrations and hands-on coding, ensuring participants develop both intuition and implementation skills. The schedule further integrates keynote talks, faculty interaction, and research-oriented sessions, fostering inquiry, discussion, and critical thinking. By the end of the workshop, participants will be equipped to reason about generative model behaviour, explore application directions, and begin adapting these models to problems in their domains.

Program Objectives

  • Introduce Generative AI from fundamentals to practical model families.
  • Build conceptual and practical skills through demonstrations and hands-on coding.
  • Enable research thinking via expert sessions and faculty interaction.
  • Prepare participants to identify and apply generative methods in relevant domains.

Who Should Join?

  • Students (UG/PG/Ph.D.) from CS, AI, Data Science, Electronics, and related fields.
  • Faculty members seeking to enrich teaching or research with Generative AI concepts.
  • Researchers exploring conceptual foundations and emerging applications in Generative AI.
  • Professionals wanting a structured introduction to Generative AI with practical exposure.
  • AI enthusiasts looking to learn from first principles (no advanced prerequisites required).

Benefits to Participants

  • Gain practical experience in implementing Generative AI models through guided coding labs and hands-on demonstrations.
  • Build a solid conceptual foundation in modern Generative AI model families and application workflows.
  • Strengthen employability for roles in AI/ML engineering, applied research, data science, and model development teams.
  • Develop confidence to experiment with text, image, and multimodal generation tasks using contemporary tools.
  • Learn how to design structured experiments, evaluate generative models, and interpret outputs effectively.
  • Interact with experts, engage with peers, and expand professional and academic networks within the IIT Jodhpur ecosystem.
  • Establish a foundation for further research in efficient generative models, diffusion models, and adaptation in data-limited environments.

Prerequisites

  • Laptop with Python installed (Anaconda or Google Colab access recommended).
  • Basic programming familiarity (Python preferred) - recommended but not mandatory.
  • No prior Generative AI experience required; the program starts from fundamentals.
  • Willingness to participate in interactive labs and collaborative learning.
